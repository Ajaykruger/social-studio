# Crystal Clawz Brand Context

## Brand

Crystal Clawz is a South African professional nail product brand for working nail technicians, salon owners, and nail product buyers.

The brand voice should feel:
- Friendly
- Professional
- Practical
- Confident
- Salon-focused
- Clear enough for South African Grade 10 English

Avoid luxury fluff, vague hype, and fake urgency. The message should help a nail technician quickly understand why the product is useful in real salon work.

## Core Audience

Primary audience:
- South African nail technicians
- Salon owners
- Nail product resellers and buyers
- Students or newer technicians building a professional kit

Audience needs:
- Products that are easy to understand and explain to clients
- Practical application benefits
- Good-looking content for reels, service menus, and product education
- Clear calls to action without exaggerated claims

## Default Content Rules

Every script should:
- Open with the product benefit in the first 3 seconds
- Use short spoken lines
- Prefer one clear idea per scene
- Include simple visual direction when useful
- Keep claims grounded in approved product information
- End with a direct shopping or enquiry call to action

Do not:
- Invent testimonials, awards, lab results, reviews, or before-and-after proof
- Make medical or health claims
- Promise perfect results
- Say a product works for every client unless verified
- Mention Shopify, Xero, ERP, internal systems, API keys, or automation tools
- Sound like a generic dropshipping advert

## French Rubber Base Positioning

Use this direction for French Rubber Base content:
- A smooth base helps colour or French work look cleaner
- Useful for overlays, base work, tip extensions, form sculpting, and deco adhesion where the product page supports that
- Speak to nail technicians, not general consumers
- Keep the message practical: base, structure, flexibility, salon workflow, and neat-looking results

Approved phrases:
- "smooth base"
- "cleaner colour application"
- "salon-ready base"
- "for nail technicians"
- "French sets"
- "overlay work"
- "shop Crystal Clawz"

Use TPO-free wording only when the selected product or collection page supports it.

## Visual Direction

Prefer:
- Product bottle close-ups
- Shade range or colour swatches
- Clean salon table styling
- Soft nude, pink, white, and black accents
- Large product image, low text
- Clear CTA end frame

Avoid:
- Too many bullet points
- Tiny product images
- Busy backgrounds
- Medical-looking visuals
- Fake client hands or fake before-and-after results unless real approved footage is supplied
